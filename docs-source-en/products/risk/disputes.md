### 3. Chargeback alert (Chargeback Alert)

> Provide a golden handling window before the risk solidifies into a formal case, achieving the most cost-effective risk resolution.

#### 3.1 Scenario

When a cardholder questions a credit card transaction and contacts the issuer to complain, but the bank has not yet formally initiated the Chargeback process, card schemes or third-party networks (such as Visa RDR and Mastercard Ethoca) generate an alert signal. At this point, the transaction is in a non-mandatory negotiation window. The Merchant has **24–72 hours** of golden handling time and can proactively intervene to prevent the case from being formally created.

#### 3.2 Core capabilities

- **Multi-channel alert intake**: Integrates with mainstream card-scheme alert networks such as Visa RDR and Mastercard Ethoca to enable standardized intake and centralized management of risk signals.
- **Intelligent order matching**: Automatically and accurately matches Merchant orders using multi-dimensional signals such as transaction identifier, amount, time, and masked card number; supports manual assisted matching as a supplement.
- **Automated handling strategies**: Supports rule-based automated actions, such as automatically issuing refunds after an alert is triggered, maximizing the window to prevent Chargebacks.
- **End-to-end visibility**: Provides dedicated dashboards and case lists to track alert status, refund progress, and outcomes in real time; exceptions can receive manual intervention support.

#### 3.3 Merchant value

- **Prevent loss escalation**: Resolve issues at the stage of zero penalties and zero record, fundamentally avoiding subsequent high Chargeback costs and account Risk Control exposure.
- **Improve operational efficiency**: Automated strategies and intelligent matching greatly reduce manual investigation and response time, ensuring handling is completed within the limited window.
- **Protect account health**: Successfully resolving alerts prevents Chargeback rates from rising and preserves the Merchant’s reputation rating with card schemes and Acquiring institutions.

### 4. Dispute (Customer Dispute)

> During the bank case inquiry stage, submit compliant, professional evidence to clarify facts and prevent the case from moving into funds debiting.

#### 4.1 Scenario

A formal information inquiry process initiated by the issuer or a payment Channel. The bank requires the Merchant to provide transaction evidence to verify transaction authenticity; funds are typically not yet debited at this point. This is the negotiation and evidence clarification stage—responding to Dispute requests promptly and accurately can successfully stop the case from escalating.

#### 4.2 Core capabilities

- **Intelligent evidence management**: Automatically aggregates relevant transaction data, logistics records, and user authorization proof, and generates a standardized response package with one click.
- **Smart deadline reminders**: Based on response deadlines across different card schemes (typically 7–20 business days), automatically sends reminders to avoid losing rights due to late response.
- **Unified multi-channel management**: Manage Dispute cases across Visa, Mastercard, and local payment Channels in a single Dashboard—no need to switch between platforms.
- **Case status tracking**: Displays case progress in real time, including bank feedback and the latest status updates.

#### 4.3 Merchant value

- **Reduce escalation probability**: Effective Dispute handling can significantly reduce the likelihood of escalation into a formal Chargeback, lowering overall losses.
- **Lower manual cost**: Automated evidence collection and standardized submission significantly reduce manual processing time.
- **Compliance assurance**: Ensures responses are submitted within card-scheme time limits, preventing automatic escalation due to non-response.

### 5. Chargeback (Chargeback)

> At the final stage of risk handling, recover funds through systematic evidence-based representment and reduce overall losses.

#### 5.1 Scenario

After a cardholder or issuer raises a formal objection to a transaction, the issuer initiates a formal process to forcibly debit the disputed amount from the Merchant’s account. This process follows clear rules and deadlines and incurs processing fees.

<Note>
The total cost of a single Chargeback is typically 2–3x the order amount, including the refunded amount, Chargeback processing fees, labor costs, and account risk losses.
</Note>

#### 5.2 Core capabilities

- **Intelligent representment strategy**: Based on case type (fraud, merchandise not received, merchandise not as described, etc.), the system recommends the optimal representment strategy and evidence combination.
- **One-click representment package submission**: Automatically generates representment materials in formats compliant with each card scheme, including transaction records, proof of delivery, communication logs, and more.
- **Win-rate tracking and analysis**: Analyzes historical win rates, identifies high-risk product categories and buyer behavior patterns, and supports Merchants in optimizing business decisions.
- **Funds recovery monitoring**: Tracks representment progress and recovery status in real time, and notifies Merchants immediately when a decision outcome is issued.

#### 5.3 Merchant value

- **Increase fund recovery rate**: Professional representment strategies and comprehensive evidence can significantly improve win rates and effectively recover debited funds.
- **Reduce operating costs**: Automation and intelligence reduce manual effort and lower per-case handling cost.
- **Account compliance assurance**: Keep Chargeback rates below card-scheme thresholds (typically 1%) to ensure normal account operations.

### Frequently asked questions (FAQ)

**Q: What is the difference between a Chargeback alert and a Chargeback?**

A: A Chargeback alert is an early signal before the bank formally opens a case and is the lowest-cost stage to handle; a Chargeback is a formal process where the issuer has already enforced a debit, and the overall cost is higher once it reaches this stage. It is recommended to handle alerts promptly to avoid escalation into a Chargeback.

**Q: How long do I have to respond after receiving a Dispute notice?**

A: Time limits vary by card scheme and are generally 7–20 business days. The Waffo system will automatically remind you of cases approaching deadlines to ensure you do not lose representment rights due to late response.

**Q: What types of evidence are most effective?**

A: For physical goods orders, the most effective evidence includes proof-of-delivery/signature records and communications showing the buyer confirmed receipt. For digital goods, this includes IP address records, download/access logs, and user authorization proof.

**Q: How long do I have to respond after receiving a dispute notice?**

A: For physical goods, the most effective evidence includes delivery confirmation records and buyer acknowledgment communications. For digital goods, IP address logs, download/access logs, and user authorization credentials are most compelling.
