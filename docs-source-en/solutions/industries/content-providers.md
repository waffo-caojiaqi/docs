## Industry overview

Content providers cover a wide range of formats, including online education, news media, audio/video streaming, podcasts, e-books, and more. Their business models are typically based on paid subscriptions, one-time purchases, or tipping. With users distributed globally, merchants must provide convenient payment methods for users in different countries while ensuring content copyright protection and access control, and they must also handle complex multi-country tax compliance requirements.

## Core needs

Key challenges content merchants face:

- **Subscription management complexity**: Need to handle multiple subscription state changes such as trials, upgrades/downgrades, pauses, and cancellations
- **Payment failure handling**: High failure rates for subscription renewals require intelligent retry strategies and user payment method update flows (Dunning Management)
- **Digital services tax compliance**: When selling digital content to users in the EU, UK, Australia, and other regions, VAT/GST must be collected on behalf of the merchant
- **User experience**: Subscription entry points and checkout pages must closely match the content platform’s style and support embedded integrations
- **Global acquiring**: Support local payment methods in users’ regions to reduce churn caused by unsupported payment options
