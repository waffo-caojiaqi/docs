## Industry overview

Digital entertainment is one of the fastest-growing cross-border payment scenarios globally, covering a wide range of business models such as online games, mobile games, live-stream tipping, video streaming, and music subscriptions. Users in this industry are widely distributed, payment frequency is high, and average transaction amounts are relatively small. The industry is also extremely sensitive to a smooth payment experience—any payment friction can directly lead to user churn or abandoned top-ups.

## Core needs

Key payment challenges faced by digital entertainment merchants include:

- **High concurrency, low latency**: During promotions or major content launches, payment request volumes can spike instantly, requiring the payment system to support elastic scaling
- **Localized payment methods**: User preferences vary significantly by region, requiring support for mainstream local methods such as Line Pay, GrabPay, and PIX
- **Subscriptions and virtual currency**: Auto-renewing membership subscriptions and in-game virtual currency top-ups are core acquiring scenarios
- **Chargeback risk management**: Digital goods are difficult to prove as “delivered,” resulting in higher chargeback risk and requiring robust risk control and dispute handling mechanisms
- **Global tax compliance**: Digital services face VAT/DST compliance obligations in multiple countries
